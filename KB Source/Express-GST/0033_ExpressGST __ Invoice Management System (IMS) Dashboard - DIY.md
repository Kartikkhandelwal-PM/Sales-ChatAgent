The Invoice Management System (IMS) within ExpressGST is a comprehensive utility that enables recipients to manage and act upon invoices filed by suppliers through GSTR-1, GSTR-1A, or IFF. This feature allows users to accept, reject, or mark invoices as pending in order to accurately claim Input Tax Credit (ITC).

In addition, users have the ability to import their book data and reconcile it with GST data. Based on the reconciliation results, users can take appropriate action directly through the IMS Dashboard, ensuring compliance and accuracy in GST filings.

IMS Workflow: Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Select Company and Access IMS Dashboard

Action: Choose the relevant company for which invoice reconciliation is to be performed. 

Step 2: Import Book Data for Reconciliation

Action: Click on the Import Book Data icon to upload the accounting records into the system.

      
System Response: The application will process the imported data and generate a detailed comparison of reconciled and unreconciled invoices, helping users easily identify mismatches.
Next Steps: Click the Sync button to refresh invoice data up to the latest available date. After synchronization, navigate to the Reconciliation section and open the IMS Dashboard to continue with the reconciliation process.

      
      

Step 3: Filter and Sort Invoices by Status

Action: Utilize the sorting filters available on the right side of the invoice table to organize data by reconciliation status. Use the No Action dropdown to choose one of the following:

      
Available Statuses:

Matched

Partially Matched

Books Only

IMS Only

                  
Purpose: Sorting by status streamlines the identification of variances and assists in efficient action-taking.

Step 4: Review and Take Action on Invoices

Action: Select the checkbox beside one or multiple invoices that require action.
Next Step: Use the No Action dropdown to choose one of the following actions:
Accept – Confirm the invoice as valid.
Reject – Mark the invoice as invalid or incorrect.
Mark as Pending – Defer the decision for further review.

                  
Purpose: These actions update the invoice status in the system for accurate tracking and ITC reconciliation.

Step 5: Review Summary of Actions

Action: Return to the IMS Dashboard to view a consolidated summary of actions taken.
Summary Includes:

Total invoices accepted, rejected, or marked as pending.

                  
Purpose: Enables users to monitor the progress and outcomes of the invoice reconciliation process.

Step 6: Compute GSTR-2B

Action: Click the Compute 2B option to finalize all marked actions.

      
Outcome: This operation transfers the reconciled invoice data into the GSTR-2B report, ready for filing.

Step 7: Final Review and Confirmation

Action: After verifying the summary of reconciled invoices, click Compute 2B once again to confirm and finalize the process.

      
      
Purpose: This final step ensures that all data is accurately updated and reflected in the GSTR-2B return.
